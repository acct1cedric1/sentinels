FAQ

The five questions everyone asks first.

Q.01What is Sentinels?

A real-time analytics terminal tracking the most successful wallets on Solana. Instead of buying wallet labels from a third party, we discover smart money directly from on-chain swaps — the most active traders across the chain's traded tokens, scored by their actual realized win rate. You see what they buy, what they sell, and how good they really are.

Q.02How do you decide which wallets are smart money?

Every refresh we scan the most-active tokens chain-wide, parse their recent swaps on-chain, and aggregate every active trader. Each wallet is then scored by win rate — the share of its closed trades that were profitable, computed from its own swap history using an average-cost basis. High win rate + meaningful volume = smart money. A transparent, recomputed-live heuristic, not a static list.

Q.03Is connecting my wallet safe?

Yes. Connecting only asks your wallet to sign a message — never a transaction. A message signature costs nothing, can't move funds, and can't approve anything. It simply proves you control the wallet so the server can check your token balance. Every Solana wallet is supported (Phantom, Solflare, Backpack, OKX, Coinbase and more) via the Wallet Standard.

Q.04What do I unlock by holding the token?

Win rates are free for everyone. Holding the required amount of our token unlocks the identities of elite wallets — their addresses and the coins they're trading — plus every future holder-gated feature on the roadmap. Verification is on-chain and enforced server-side: the data simply never leaves the server for non-holders.

Q.05Where does the data come from, and how fresh is it?

Everything is computed from public on-chain data: swaps parsed via Helius, token discovery and prices from GeckoTerminal, pump.fun launches from pump.fun's public feed. The flow board refreshes every 5 minutes; the launch radar refreshes every few seconds and includes tokens created moments ago. Analytics, not financial advice — memecoins are extremely high-risk.
